From the Editor

The opportunity to build a modern home, on the right piece of land, at a fraction of the cost.

Across the four Pacific-facing submarkets we follow — Altadena, Pacific Palisades, Brentwood and Malibu — the residential land market is unusually transparent right now. Asking prices are visible. Lot sizes are documented. Prior structures are a matter of public record. For buyers prepared to build, this is one of the most legible land markets the region has produced in a generation.

This edition lays out current asking inventory across all four submarkets, with lot sizes, prior-structure context, and a map pin for every parcel. The opportunity for the patient buyer is straightforward: select a lot in an established neighborhood, build to a modern footprint and a modern standard, and capture the discount between buying a finished home and building a better one. We have organized the data so the comparison is easy to make.

III · THE RECOVERY ECONOMY
Field Report

Who is building. Who is weighing. Who is moving on.

An average is the sum of many decisions. Here is the broader picture beneath the asking prices — sentiment, pace and the patterns shaping inventory across all four submarkets.

The pattern beneath the prices

Across both fire-affected submarkets, the visible groundbreakings are not the median — but they are the leading indicator. The largest cohort remains the homeowner still weighing options: insurance status, contractor capacity, and a three-year build window are the three variables doing most of the work.

  • Construction underwayMore than 1,000 Altadena homes under construction; a smaller but growing number of Palisades projects framing.
  • The undecided cohortNearly 80% of Palisades homeowners surveyed indicate intent to rebuild — most contingent on a three-year window.
  • Insurance gapA material share of owners face a gap between settlement and rebuild cost — for some, this becomes the decision point.
  • Inventory dynamicsLand inventory has expanded materially in Malibu and the Palisades; Altadena remains the most rationally-priced of the four markets; Brentwood barely trades.

IV.

Where We Are Looking

Strategic notes
The Opportunity

In-tract Altadena buildable parcels at or below $700K — particularly where adjacent owners have already broken ground. Palisades parcels in the $1.5M–$2.5M band that have been on the market long enough to engage a serious offer. Malibu Las Flores and Big Rock mesa lots where the asking has moved meaningfully from initial list.

The thesis is straightforward: select a parcel in an established neighborhood, build to a modern footprint and a modern energy and seismic standard, and capture the discount versus a comparable finished home.

What We Look For

Clean Chapter 7A clearance, completed debris removal certificate, no unresolved insurance entanglement, and meaningful evidence of neighbors who are actively building. These are the markers of a parcel that will trade efficiently and a neighborhood that will recover meaningfully.

The Brentwood opportunity is narrower but distinct: scarce virgin Westside land that simply does not come to market in volume. Patience is the entire strategy.
