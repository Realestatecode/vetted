The Market

Abbot Kinney was famously dubbed “The Coolest Block in America,” and Summer 2026 has not dulled it: the boulevard still pulls a high-spend mix of local creatives, tourists, and weekend day-trippers. Recent neighborhood arrivals — RVR, Barrique, Market, La Isla Bonita — have re-energized the strip’s independent dining identity.

The Space

Two-level corridor property at the center of Abbot Kinney, two blocks from the shoreline. Upper level offers a complete kitchen with three flexible rooms and bath; ground floor is a white-box brand gallery flowing into a private sunlit courtyard. Configurable as a single turnkey hospitality footprint or split into restaurant + retail activation.

The Market

Downtown Culver City is in the middle of its strongest hospitality cycle in a decade. IKEA’s smaller-format city-center store opened at Helms in Spring 2026, anchoring a wave of new tenants — Hayama by WATAMI (flame-grilled yakitori & sushi) and Folks Pizzeria (OC import). Studios + creative-office demand (Amazon, Apple-adjacent, HBO/Max) continues to feed the dinner check. Culver Public Market at Washington & Centinela restarted construction with Smog City Brewery and Churrería El Moro signed.

The Space

A storied second-generation restaurant + bar + marketplace inside Culver City’s historic Hull Building (1924). Full hood, dual kitchen lines, bar program build-out, marketplace retail frontage. Footprint configures cleanly as a 70–110 seat dinner room with a bar, or as a hybrid dinner + market concept.

The Market

Main Street is Santa Monica’s most consistent neighborhood corridor — high local repeat, walkable from Ocean Park and Sunset Park, and insulated from the Promenade’s chain churn. Recent activity: two Main Street eateries closed in early 2026; a Canada-based dessert chain has taken the former Holey Grail Donuts space; Vivibowl is planning a take-out room on the nearby Third Street Promenade. Operators are choosing this corridor for community over foot-traffic.

The Space

The opportunity is a small cluster of recently-vacated 2nd-gen rooms in the 2700–3000 block of Main Street. Footprints typically 1,500–3,500 SF with hood + kitchen in place, frequently with patio rights leveraged under the city’s Al Fresco ordinance.

The Market

Mar Vista quietly became one of the Westside’s most interesting food neighborhoods. Fatty Mart — Chef David Kuo’s transformation of the old Grand View Market into a multi-concept space (Fatty Slice + Korean BBQ plates) — anchors the corridor, with Hatchet Hall, Little Fatty, Taverna at the Mar Vista, Rasselbock, and N17 The Lane within walking distance. Sunday Mar Vista Farmers Market (3826 Grand View) sits steps away.

The Space

A small-format 2nd-gen footprint on the 3800 block of Grand View, near established Italian + pasta operators. Hood and basic kitchen line in place; configuration suits an owner-operator chef who wants a 30–50 seat room, walk-up counter, or a tight bar-driven concept.

The Market

The Santa Monica Airport non-aviation property pool sits in a unique pocket of the Westside — adjacent to Penmar, Sunset Park, and the northern edge of Venice, with a creative-tenant base that has long drawn coffee, bakery, and chef-driven F&B users. City of Santa Monica continues to publish active commercial leasing opportunities on city-owned property; the area’s creative-office density (architecture, design, post-production) drives a strong daytime daypart.

The Space

Representative opportunity is a 2nd-gen F&B unit within the city-managed inventory or in a Penmar-edge private building, with existing hood + kitchen retained from a prior cafe / catering / chef-driven concept. Suits an operator who wants creative-class lunch + early dinner volume without paying Promenade rents.
